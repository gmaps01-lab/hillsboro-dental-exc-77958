Frequently Asked Questions About Sleep Apnea

How much does a sleep apnea oral appliance cost at Hillsboro Dental Excellence, and is it covered by insurance?
At Hillsboro Dental Excellence, the cost for a custom sleep apnea oral appliance typically ranges from $2,000 to $3,500, depending on your specific needs and the type of device recommended. Many dental and medical insurance plans—including some Oregon-based providers—may cover part or all of the cost, especially if you have a formal sleep apnea diagnosis. Our team will help you check your benefits and handle the insurance paperwork. We also offer flexible payment plans for out-of-pocket expenses.
How long does it take to get fitted for a sleep apnea oral appliance, and what is the process like?
The process usually takes about 2 to 4 weeks from your initial consultation to receiving your custom appliance. During your first visit, Dr. Skinner will review your sleep study results or refer you for a home sleep study if you haven’t had one. We’ll take digital impressions of your teeth to ensure a precise fit. Once your appliance is ready, you’ll return for a fitting and adjustment appointment. We schedule a follow-up after a few weeks to ensure the device is working well and make any needed tweaks.
Is Dr. Skinner at Hillsboro Dental Excellence licensed to treat sleep apnea, and what special training does the team have?
Yes, Dr. Skinner is a licensed dentist in Oregon with advanced training in dental sleep medicine. He is a member of the American Academy of Dental Sleep Medicine and regularly completes continuing education specific to sleep apnea and oral appliance therapy. Our team stays up to date on the latest protocols and works closely with local sleep physicians to ensure coordinated care.
What kind of results can I expect from a dental appliance for sleep apnea, and is there any guarantee it will work?
Most patients with mild to moderate obstructive sleep apnea see significant improvement in symptoms—such as reduced snoring and better sleep quality—when using a custom oral appliance. While no treatment can offer a 100% guarantee due to individual differences, we closely monitor your progress and can make adjustments to optimize results. If you don’t experience improvement, we’ll work with you and your sleep physician to explore alternative solutions. We are committed to your satisfaction and follow evidence-based practices for the best possible outcome.
How does getting a sleep apnea oral appliance at your Hillsboro office compare to using a CPAP machine?
Many patients in Hillsboro find oral appliances more comfortable and easier to use than CPAP machines, especially for mild to moderate sleep apnea. The appliance is custom-fitted, portable, and doesn’t require electricity or a mask. It’s a popular choice for patients who have trouble tolerating CPAP or travel frequently. We’ll review your medical history and sleep study results to determine if an oral appliance is appropriate for you, and we can coordinate with your sleep physician if you’re considering switching from CPAP therapy.
